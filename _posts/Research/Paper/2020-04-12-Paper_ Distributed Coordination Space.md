##### 3.一些概念
- **Coordination space**
<br>即坐标空间。Distributed interaction occurs over time and distance.
- **Socio-technical(社会技术的)方法**
<br>对分布式团队研究以便于一个人能够解释合作位置的缺少的认知结果和还有社交结果影响交互和团队发展当工作是技术中介的。
- [**Sociotechnical system(STS)**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sociotechnical_system)
<br>An approach to complex organizational work design that recognizes the interaction between people and technology in workplaces
- [**Distributed teams**](https://learn.g2.com/managing-distributed-team)
<br>Also called virtual teams. A distributed team is a group of coworkers who work remotely, either nearby or far away. They may work in the same city, but from home or internationally. 
<br>**Related words**: distributed interaction, distributed environments, distributed team tasks, co-located teams
- [**Workspace awareness**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Situation_awareness)
<br>Situational awareness or situation awareness (SA) is the perception of environmental elements and events with respect to time or space, the comprehension of their meaning, and the projection of their future status
- [**Telepresence**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telepresence)
<br>Telepresence refers to a set of technologies which allow a person to feel as if they were present, to give the appearance of being present, or to have an effect, via telerobotics, at a place other than their true location.
- [**Social presence theory**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_presence_theory)
<br>Defined Social Presence Theory as the ability communication media have to transmit social cues.
- **Team opacity**
<br>team members must deal with an increased level of abstraction forced upon them by their distributed nature

Within the context of a socio-technological framework labelled a coordination space and describe how the behaviors,
attitudes and cognition of team members at the intra- and inter-individual level
may be affected by a lack of co-location

- coordination decrement in distributed team environments

---

##### Team opacity and distributed team work structure

At the level of the individual, when a team is co-located, interaction is transparent in that one is more easily able to assess a teammate's actions
At the level of team, interaction behaviors normally clear may become similarly opaque.
---
