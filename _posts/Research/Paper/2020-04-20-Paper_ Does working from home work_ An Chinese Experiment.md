## Abstract
Lead to “shirking from home”. 
1.	Is it a useful management practice?
2.	The second issue relates to the concerns over deteriorating work-life balance in the US
Wfh: reduce office rental costs. 
But the executives worried that allowing employees to work at home, away from the supervision of their team leaders, would lead to a large increase in shirking.

## Striking results: 
- The performance of home workers went up dramatically. Increasing by 13% over the nine months of the experiment. (work time goes up) 
This was due to a reduction in breaks and sick-days taken by the home workers
- Quiet working conditions at home.
- there appear to be no spillovers to the rest of the group
- attrition fell sharply among the home worker
- It reduced rates of promotion by about 50%
   - Attrition 摩擦
   - Downside 下降趋势
   - Control group 是不接受实验处理的被试组

## Concerns with these results:
- Quality was not sacrificed for quantity by the home workers. 
- The results weren’t driven by the control workers’ becoming frustrated by losing the randomization lottery and performing worse.
- Our results are driven by attrition bias.

## Saving
This experiment saved about $2000 per year per employee working at home.
- Lead it to offer the option to work from home to the entire firm.
- This learning and re-selection led to the impact from working at home rising to 22%, 

## The Experimental Design
- Location: in the airfare and hotel booking departments in the Shanghai call center of CTrip, a 16000-employee, NASDAQ-listed Chinese travel agency.
- Treatment group: Operate from home for 4 of 5 shifts: work from home from 9am to 5pm on Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday and Friday and in the office from 9am to 5pm on Thursday.
- Control group: work in the office from 9am to 5pm on all five days

- Not allowed to work overtime outside their team shift, not allowed to work in the commuting time, which was 80 minutes a day for the average employee.
Individuals were not able to work overtime on their own.
### participants
- 503 volunteered for the experiment, 249 of the employees met the eligibility requirements
- 49% of employees did not opt to work from home. Despite the considerable saving in commuting time and cost. The loneliness of working from home and the lack of opportunities to socialize in the office and after work.
- Employees with an even birth date(a day ending 2, 4, 6, 8, etc.) were selected into the treatment
- Those with an odd birth date were in the control group. 
- Difference between treatment and control groups shows there was a significant difference between them in 2 of the 18 characteristics: The control group was more likely to be married and have children.

## Period
- From December 6, 2010 and lasted for 9 months, which employees knew in advance.
- After the experiment, we see about 50% of the treatment group immediately decided to return to the office, despite having to incur the financial and time costs of commuting. 
35% moved home when they were allowed to do so. The main reasons both groups gave in interviews for changing their minds were concerns over being isolated at home.
10% of the workers who did not initially volunteer changed their minds after the experiment and decided to work from home.
Employees reported that after working from home for a few months they became lonely and wanted to return to the office. 
They place a high value on social interactions at work.
